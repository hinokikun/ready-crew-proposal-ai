# ProposalPilot V39.1 Premium Visual System

## Concept

ProposalPilot Premium Visual System v39.1 makes the proposal memorable by giving every page one clear visual lead. The design goal is not decorative polish alone. The deck should show AI doing useful work while keeping the final decision human-centered.

## Color

- Deep Navy: executive pages, architecture, and high-impact visual moments.
- Royal Blue: primary ProposalPilot action color and process emphasis.
- Cyan: AI recognition frame, active data flow, and scan highlights.
- White: clarity surfaces for summaries, dashboards, and decisions.
- Pale Blue: operational context and explanatory pages.
- Green / Amber: limited state colors for approval, confirmation, and items requiring agreement.

Rules:

- Each page limits dominant colors to two or three.
- Cyan is reserved for AI recognition, active flow, and key emphasis.
- Dark backgrounds are used only on pages where visual impact or system depth matters.
- Light backgrounds are used for reading, planning, and decision pages.

## Typography

- Cover title: large, high-contrast, and outcome-oriented.
- Slide titles: concise claims, not generic section names.
- Body copy: short and presenter-friendly.
- Tags and UI labels: compact but readable.
- No dense footnotes or tiny explanatory text are used as a workaround for crowded layouts.

## Spacing

- Outer margins are kept wide.
- Each slide has one main visual area and a clear supporting text area.
- Cards and UI objects do not touch the slide edge.
- White space is used to separate message, visual, and decision cues.

## Recognition Frame

The AI Recognition Frame is the signature visual device. It uses:

- Four-corner focus marks.
- Cyan stroke.
- Floating tags.
- Candidate classifications.
- Human confirmation status.

It appears on the cover, architecture, KPI, and decision pages to tie the story together.

## Floating Tags

Tags are used for recognition outcomes and decision states. They remain editable PowerPoint pills and are not rasterized.

Tag types:

- Recognition candidate
- Review state
- Required agreement
- Measurement state
- Scope decision

## Connectors

Connectors are shown as visible data-flow rails rather than thin decorative arrows. They indicate:

- Input to AI processing.
- AI candidate to human review.
- Human approval to integration.
- Feedback back into learning operations.

## Cards

Cards are used sparingly. They support reading but do not become the main design pattern. V39.1 avoids repeating a simple card grid across the deck.

## UI Mock

The Human Review UI mock shows the expected operator experience:

- AI presents candidates.
- Human checks, corrects, and approves.
- The confirmed result becomes integration data and learning data.

## Shadows and Depth

Depth is expressed through layered surfaces, background mass, stacked panels, and foreground recognition frames. Shadows are subtle and structural rather than decorative.

## Page Background Rhythm

- Slide 1: dark hero with layered AI recognition scene.
- Slide 2: light executive summary with central human-centered message.
- Slide 3: light workflow map with load accumulation.
- Slide 4: dark before/after transformation lane.
- Slide 5: dark system architecture with central engine.
- Slide 6: white roadmap for decision clarity.
- Slide 7: pale dashboard for KPI evaluation.
- Slide 8: white decision and scope page with dark budget gate.

## ProposalPilot Signature

- ProposalPilot footer and page number.
- Cyan recognition frame.
- Floating classification tags.
- Rounded but restrained geometry.
- Human-in-the-loop visual language.
- Explicit separation between AI candidate work and human decision work.
